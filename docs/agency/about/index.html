About

Most of what I learn from AI-assisted work still wants to live in chat scrollback, a throwaway script, or hallway context. The Agency is my counterweight: a public shelf where those lessons have to survive a reread six months later.

My portfolio is credentials and case studies—stakes, options, outcomes. The Agency is the layer underneath: what I actually run (commands, checks, failure modes) after a bad deploy or a tool update.

What you’ll find #

  • Dispatches — field notes from practice: what held up, what broke, what I’d do differently when the tools ship again.
  • Playbooks — runnable how-tos with commands, checks, and failure modes named so you can skip the debugging tax I already paid.

Two tracks: Builder (Claude Code, skills, hooks, orchestration) and Desktop (Claude as a daily companion, Cowork, connectors).

Why it exists #

“How does the deploy work?” and “which skill handles this?” still default to oral tradition. Publishing forces me to test the steps, pin the paths, and refresh when reality drifts.

I’m aiming for a system that holds the knowledge—not for it to stay trapped in one person’s head.
