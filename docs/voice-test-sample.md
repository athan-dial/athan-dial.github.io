## REWRITTEN VERSION (Athan's Authentic Voice)

```markdown
## From Bench Science to Decision Systems

My PhD wasn't about biology — it was about decision-making under resource constraints. The questions I tackled weren't "what does this gene do?" They were: Which experiments maximize learning per dollar when sequencing budgets are fixed? How do we design validation frameworks from first principles when no playbook exists? What counts as "sufficient evidence" when stakeholders disagree on the underlying question?

Those aren't molecular biology problems. They're **decision architecture problems** — the same problems I solve now in drug discovery ML. The domain changed (PhD: multi-omics pipelines → Montai: compound nomination systems), but the **decision-making muscle** stayed the same: first-principles thinking about evidence, explicit tradeoffs, and evaluation frameworks that prevent metric theater.

At Montai, I design decision systems for ambiguous, high-stakes problems where "good" isn't obvious. I build the infrastructure that turns data into defensible decisions: North star + guardrails frameworks, evaluation systems combining offline validation with online monitoring, and stakeholder alignment forums with clear ownership. The throughline from PhD to product is simple — I design the systems that help teams make better decisions faster.
```

### Voice Improvements:
- ✅ **Em-dash definition** (line 1: "wasn't about biology — it was about...")
- ✅ **Rhetorical questions** (lines 1-2: actual decision questions from PhD)
- ✅ **Signature phrases** (5 used):
  - "decision-making under resource constraints"
  - "first principles"
  - "decision architecture"
  - "metric theater"
  - "North star + guardrails"
- ✅ **Compound parallel clauses** (line 6: "The domain changed... but the muscle stayed")
- ✅ **Colon-introduced list** (line 10: "North star + guardrails frameworks, evaluation systems...")
- ✅ **Confident, direct tone** - no hedging, clear statements
- ✅ **Evidence-backed** - specific examples from actual work
