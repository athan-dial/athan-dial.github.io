Case Studies

  • AI Adoption Needs a Measurement Layer — Building a repeatable adoption system with onboarding, evidence, and iteration instead of one-time rollouts.
  • Competitive Intelligence Runs on Identity — Building a canonical entity resolution system as the foundation for competitive intelligence, proving that data identity—not visualization—was the blocker.
  • Data Quality Is an Incident Discipline — Building incident response protocols and lineage-aware monitoring transformed silent data failures into defensible decisions in a drug discovery pipeline.
  • Decision Intelligence Needs Structure and Nerve — Rebuilding the decision capture layer—analytics, compliance, and publication architecture—so high-stakes compound selections traced back to verifiable data, not oral tradition.
  • Influence Comes From Decision Infrastructure — How reframing a reactive data team as a strategic decision intelligence function transformed organizational impact through governance, named research portfolios, and quantitative prioritization frameworks.
  • Product Signals for Scientific Tools — Installing product discipline into a research organization where tools were specced in Slack and launched without adoption plans, using lightweight PRDs, project posters, and adoption tracking to accelerate shipping and clarify tradeoffs.
  • Similarity Decisions Need Weighted Frameworks — How a weighted decision matrix converted a contentious technology debate into a transparent artifact, turning opinions into evidence and preserving architectural flexibility.
  • Training Data as Product: Building a Reusable Dataset Pipeline — I built a 10,000-compound training dataset from patent and public database sources, designing it for stable ingestion, repeatable consumption, and defensible review.
  • Warehouse Discipline Without a Data Team — Scaling warehouse governance and data lineage through dbt on Athena, enabling faster decision-making without additional headcount.
  • The Shadow Process Problem: Systematizing a Scientific Workflow — A biologist was running mechanism-of-action analysis through ChatGPT and Excel. The process worked. It was also unreproducible, unauditable, and about to break under a second collaboration. The fix started with an intake session, not a tool.
  • 100+ Internal Tools and No Way to Know Which Ones Matter — Over 100 internal software tools sat in a catalog tagged 'active' or 'inactive.' That binary told leadership nothing about which ones would cause a crisis if they broke. I designed a two-axis risk model to make the exposure visible.
  • Reducing Data Pipeline Latency to Enable Real-Time Decision Loops — Redesigned batch pipeline to streaming architecture, reducing data lag from 2-3 days to <2 hours and enabling new decision workflows
  • Preventing Metric Theater in Drug Discovery ML — Designed evaluation framework combining offline validation with online performance tracking to de-risk high-stakes compound selection decisions
  • Build vs Buy: Strategic Analysis for Analog Generation — Quantitative analysis guiding a strategic analog generation partnership decision — ROI modeling revealed internal model needed significant accuracy improvement, recommended hybrid approach balancing near-term progress with long-term IP
  • Learning Agendas: Bringing Research Rigor to Product Decisions — Designed decision framework cutting R&D cycle time 20% by pre-defining success criteria and pivot triggers — applied academic experimental design to product strategy
  • From Data Crisis to Data Culture: A Critical Program Incident — Led 6-week crisis response resolving data integrity failure — transformed incident into governance framework reducing future incidents from 3 to 0
  • Standardizing Montai's App Ecosystem with R Shiny — Converged fragmented app development onto single framework, cutting development time 66% — balanced team skills with deployment simplicity to accelerate internal tool velocity
  • A Biologist Caught What No Automated System Did — Two model configurations that should have behaved differently were producing nearly identical outputs. The anomaly was caught by a domain expert, not a data system. The root cause traced through four handoffs where no stage validated what the previous one promised.
  • Operating Discipline in Compound Nominations — Building a structured, auditable nomination system that turned ad-hoc target selection into an operating rhythm, improving hit rate enrichment and decision traceability across three years and five-plus biological targets.
  • Scaling AI-Driven Drug Nominations from 250 to 7,000 Compounds — Built pipeline scaling compound nominations 26× with measurably improved hit rates — phased rollout strategy balanced speed with quality gates
